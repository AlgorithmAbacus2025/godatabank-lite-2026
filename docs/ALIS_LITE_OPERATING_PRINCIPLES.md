# ALIS-Lite Operating Principles

## Core principle

Breaking stories are signals, not evidence.

Official sources are evidence.

Analysis begins only after the evidence chain exists.

## Meaning

A breaking story may trigger a research question, but it must not be treated as sufficient evidence for a GoDataBank Lite report.

Reports must be grounded in official sources, primary documents, public datasets, APIs, institutional records, or directly attributable statements.

## Workflow gates

No topic may move from story scan to source planning unless a clear research question exists.

No topic may move from source planning to fetch unless official or primary sources are identified.

No topic may move from fetch to analysis unless raw evidence and source records exist.

No topic may move from analysis to report unless claim boundaries are recorded.

No topic may move from draft report to public site unless verification and publication gate approval exist.

## Role mapping

ALIS: source discovery, source planning, and official-source fetch.

Thoth: raw evidence, source records, provenance, and verification storage.

Abacus: analysis, classification, claim control, and draft reporting.

Gatekeeper: verification before publication.

Publisher: static public output only after gate approval.
