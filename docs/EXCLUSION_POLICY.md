# Exclusion Policy

## Purpose

The exclusion policy protects GoDataBank from unsupported, unofficial, commercial, or commentary-based evidence. Excluded sources must not be used for admission, verification, report content, charts, tables, summaries, or background claims.

## Always Excluded

The following source categories are excluded:

- News organisations
- Commercial data vendors
- Think tanks
- Blogs
- Social media platforms and posts
- User-generated content
- Advocacy organisations
- Lobbying organisations
- Political campaign sites
- Personal websites
- Forums and community discussion sites
- Unofficial mirrors
- Unofficial aggregators
- Search-engine snippets
- AI-generated answers
- Unsourced copied tables
- Screenshots without official structured source data
- Human-only pages without a public API or approved machine-readable feed

## Vendor and Aggregator Rule

Commercial data vendors and unofficial aggregators remain excluded even when they reproduce official data. GoDataBank must cite the official institution and its machine-readable source directly.

## News and Commentary Rule

News articles, media explainers, opinion pieces, newsletters, and commentary pages are excluded even when they discuss official data. They may not be used as evidence, confirmation, or citation support.

## Think Tank Rule

Think tanks and research institutes outside official public institutional authority are excluded. This applies even when their work is data-rich, methodologically detailed, or publicly accessible.

## Social Media Rule

Social media content is excluded even when posted by an official account. Use the official institution website, API, open data portal, publication catalogue, or structured feed instead.

## Machine-Readable Failure

A source is excluded from admission when it lacks a public API or approved machine-readable feed. Human-readable pages alone are not sufficient for this milestone.

## English Failure

A source is excluded from admission when relevant content or metadata is not available in English.

## Conflict Resolution

If a source appears both official and excluded, the stricter rule applies until the official institution's direct machine-readable source is identified and cited.
