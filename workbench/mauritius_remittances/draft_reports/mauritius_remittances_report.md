# Mauritius: Personal Remittances Received as a Percentage of GDP

## Report status

Draft report. Not yet published.

## Research question

How significant are personal remittances received in Mauritius when measured as a percentage of GDP, according to World Bank data?

## Findings

The World Bank series contains 31 valid annual values for Mauritius, covering 1994 to 2024.

The latest valid value is for 2024, when personal remittances received were recorded as approximately 1.92% of GDP.

The highest value in the valid series was recorded in 1998, at approximately 4.26% of GDP.

The lowest value in the valid series was recorded in 2011, at approximately 0.004% of GDP.

## Evidence-based interpretation

The data shows that personal remittances received form a measurable but relatively limited share of Mauritius GDP in the latest available year. The 2024 value of approximately 1.92% of GDP does not, by itself, support a strong claim that Mauritius is highly dependent on remittances.

The series does show historical variation, including a higher level in the late 1990s and a very low recorded value in 2011. A stronger interpretation would require comparison with other countries, additional macroeconomic indicators, or a defined threshold for remittance dependence.

## Limitations

This report uses one World Bank indicator only.

The data does not explain why values rose or fell.

The data does not separate remittance sources by country of origin.

The data does not show household-level dependence on remittances.

The 2025 API record appears but has a null value, so 2024 is treated as the latest valid year.
