## Findings

The World Bank series contains 65 valid annual values for Mauritius, covering 1960 to 2024.

The latest valid value is for 2024, when GDP per capita was recorded as approximately 11,990.78 current US$.

The highest value in the series was recorded in 2024, at approximately 11,990.78 current US$.

The lowest value in the series was recorded in 1960, at approximately 238.25 current US$.

## Evidence-based interpretation

The World Bank World Development Indicators data shows that Mauritius GDP per capita in current US dollars rose substantially from approximately 238.25 US$ in 1960 to 11,990.78 US$ in 2024.

This nominal indicator shows a long-term upward trend in the nominal dollar output allocated per person. However, because the indicator is measured in current US dollars (nominal values), this upward trend does not by itself support claims that real purchasing power has increased, that overall living standards have improved, or that economic prosperity has definitely risen for the population. Furthermore, nominal GDP per capita trends do not show changes in income inequality, nor do they establish that specific government policies caused the nominal increase. This nominal growth is affected by multiple factors, including inflation, exchange rate fluctuations, population shifts, and nominal GDP changes. The nominal trend does not classify Mauritius as a rich or high-income country without reference to defined international threshold comparisons.

## Limitations and null-value handling

This report uses one World Bank nominal indicator only.

The 2025 API record appears in the raw response but has a null value. Therefore, 2024 is treated as the latest valid year.

The data does not adjust for inflation (real values) or purchasing power parity (PPP).

The data does not show distribution of income or household wealth.
